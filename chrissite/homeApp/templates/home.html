About

Christian Eason is currently a student at the University of the Arts. He discovered his vocal abilities at the age of 10 while living with just his mother. As a baby Christian was raised by two women and currently calls both women “mom”. One of the biggest struggles for Christian was not having a father present in his life. His mother, Lenore Eason began to take on foster care, and Christian quickly became a father figure or and older brother for these young children that were coming in and out of his home. Christian uses the emotions of his father to really connect to his music and push him forward in life to one finally meet him, because his father doesn’t even know he exists.

In 2007 Christian moved to Marietta, Georgia to spend time with his only sibling Coolta Eason who has been absent from his life since he was a baby. The age differences between them two kept them separated, but she became a huge mentor for him and an inspiration. In 2013 Christian began coaching cheerleading for an All Star Gym, Dynasty Spirit Elite AllStars. He also began working as a singing busboy at the Walnut Street Supper Club in Philadelphia.

In 2009 Christian moved back to Philadelphia and with the help of mentors Virginia Lam, and Jason Ikeem Rodgers, he could pursue a life in music. He attended a music high school and soon was rewarded Musician of the year in the Philadelphia School District. Christian started studying voice with professional opera singer Reginald Pindel in 2010, and through the years also studied with Tony Award winner Jesus Garcia. In 2006 he started studying acting at the Walnut Street Theatre in Philadelphia. Christian has performed in the Philadelphia All City Choir as well as the renowned “Philadelphia Singers”. He was seen on stages with Paul Simon, Lawrence Brownlee, Terrance Howard, Renee Fleming and James Earl Jones. In 2010 he performed for president Barack Obama. Christian also performed for the Liberty Bell Medal in 2011 for Tony Blair and 2012 for Muhammad Ali.

Growing up with such a huge family who loves music, Christian has always been around people who can sing and want to pursue a life as a musician. His grandmother had 12 kids (10 girls, 2 boys). Together, they all formed a choir called the Eason Singers and they traveled all around Philadelphia singing for the community and at different churches. In addition, Christian is a two-time winner (2012, 2013) of the Marian Anderson Young Artist Award for outstanding music abilities. Eason branched out in to musical theatre starring as a lead in the shows RENT, The Sound of Music, Hairspray, Grease, Beauty and the Beast, Pippin and Parade. He also Collaborated with the company Subcircle making music for they’re hit performance “Thine Eyes”. Christian began to produce his own shows and put on concerts and recitals for the community of Philadelphia. Pushed by his mother and sister he merged into a great charismatic loving professional of the arts.
